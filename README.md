## What Makes Kratom Extract Different from Standard Kratom?

Most kratom users start with powder. Experienced users eventually discover extract. Understanding the fundamental difference is essential before buying.

Standard kratom powder is ground dried leaf. Quality powder contains 1.0% to 1.8% mitragynine by weight. A standard 5-gram dose contains approximately 50mg to 90mg of mitragynine. That's the baseline.

Kratom extract is a manufactured concentrated product. Through solvent or water-based extraction processes, the alkaloids are isolated and concentrated from the raw leaf material. The plant matter is removed. The solvent is evaporated or reduced. What remains is an alkaloid-rich concentrated extract with dramatically higher potency per gram than standard powder.

**How concentration levels work:**

- **Standard powder baseline:** 1.0% to 1.8% mitragynine
- **10x extract powder:** approximately 10% to 18% mitragynine
- **45% extract:** 45% mitragynine — roughly 25 to 45 times more concentrated than standard powder
- **Liquid extract shot (100mg):** may contain equivalent mitragynine to 6 to 10 grams of standard powder in 10ml to 15ml liquid

The concentration creates opportunity and risk simultaneously. Opportunity — more potent effects in smaller quantities. Risk — dosing errors have larger consequences, and quality fraud is financially rewarding enough to attract dishonest vendors.

**Extract-specific contamination risk:** Unlike powder, extract manufacturing introduces residual solvents as a unique contamination risk. The solvents used in extraction must be fully removed in the finished product. Incomplete removal leaves residual chemicals in a concentrated product that the user directly consumes. This is why residual solvent testing is non-negotiable for extract products specifically — and why it's often absent from basic vendor testing panels.

## How to Read a Kratom Extract COA: The Complete Guide

The Certificate of Analysis for extract must contain more information than a standard powder COA. Here's exactly what to verify.

**Finished product testing vs. source material testing**
The COA must specify that it covers the finished extract product — not the source leaf or powder before extraction. This is the most commonly missed verification step. Source leaf testing does not tell you the alkaloid concentration or solvent content of the finished extract.

**Mitragynine percentage in finished extract**
Expressed as percentage of extract material by weight. A "45% mitragynine extract" means 45% of the extract material is mitragynine. This is the most important quality number for extract products.

**7-hydroxymitragynine levels**
Must be present. Given 7-OH's 13x opioid receptor binding potency (Journal of Medicinal Chemistry, 2016), even small percentages drive significant effects. Any extract COA missing 7-OH data is incomplete.

**Residual solvent testing**
This is the extract-specific parameter that most buyers don't know to look for. The COA must show residual levels of extraction solvents (ethanol, ethyl acetate, water, or others depending on process) within safe limits. Absence of residual solvent data on an extract COA is a red flag that this critical safety parameter was not tested.

**Heavy metals panel on finished extract**
Critical distinction — heavy metals found in source leaf are concentrated during extraction. Test must be performed on the finished concentrated product, not the source material. Lead, arsenic, mercury, cadmium all within AKA thresholds.

**Microbial testing**
E. coli, salmonella, yeast, mold — particularly important for liquid extract products where microbial growth risk is higher than in dry powder products.

**Testing laboratory by name**
Specific named accredited laboratory — not generic "independently tested" claim without lab identification.

**Batch number correspondence**
Your product batch number must match the specific COA document. This is the verifiable chain of custody.

## Kratom Extract Dosing: The Most Critical Guide You'll Read

Extract dosing errors are the most common source of negative kratom experiences. This section may be the most important thing you read before purchasing.

### The Fundamental Rule

Kratom extract is not dosed the same as kratom powder. A user who takes their standard 5-gram powder dose in extract form will significantly overdose. The mitragynine per gram of extract can be 10x to 45x higher than in standard powder. Direct dose transfer from powder to extract is dangerous without recalculation.

### Conversion Reference

Standard 5-gram powder dose contains approximately 50mg to 90mg mitragynine (at 1.0% to 1.8% mitragynine).

To achieve the same dose from a 45% extract:
50mg ÷ 450mg per gram = approximately 0.11 grams of extract

This is why extract dosing is measured in fractions of a gram or in specific milligram amounts — not in the gram quantities used for powder.

### Dosing Protocol by User Level

**Powder users new to extract — mandatory protocol:**
- Begin at the minimum suggested dose on the product label
- For liquid shots: take half the bottle, wait 30 to 40 minutes
- For extract powder: start with 0.1 to 0.2 grams (100mg to 200mg)
- Wait full 45 minutes before evaluating — faster onset but still requires time to peak
- No additional dose in the first session
- Log: exact milligrams, time, effects at 20, 45, and 90 minutes

**Regular extract users:**
- Work within the product-specific milligram guidance based on verified COA concentration
- Calculate total mitragynine per dose from stated concentration and dose weight
- Adjust in small milligram increments — not percentage-of-dose jumps

**Critical extract dosing variables:**
- Empty stomach increases onset speed and intensity significantly
- Extract's faster onset means effects arrive before you've had time to decide to redose — wait before adding more
- Tolerance to extract builds faster than to powder — rotation and break schedules are essential
- Never combine with alcohol or other CNS depressants

## Why Residual Solvent Testing Is Non-Negotiable for Extract

This is the quality and safety gap that most extract buyers don't know exists — and that most vendors don't address.

Standard kratom powder manufacturing does not involve chemical solvents. The only post-harvest processing is drying and grinding. Contamination risks are limited to heavy metals, pesticides, and microbials from the growing environment.

Extract manufacturing adds chemical solvents to the process. Ethanol, ethyl acetate, acetone, or water-based systems are used to selectively extract alkaloids from plant material. These solvents must be fully removed through evaporation or distillation in the finished product.

Incomplete solvent removal — due to insufficient processing time, inadequate equipment, or poor manufacturing practices — leaves residual chemical concentrations in the finished extract. The user then directly consumes those residual solvents in a concentrated product format.

The health implications of residual solvent exposure in concentrated products are distinct from the alkaloid safety profile of the kratom itself. Residual ethanol in trace amounts may be acceptable. Residual ethyl acetate above safe limits is a different matter entirely.

This is why residual solvent testing must appear on every extract COA. And why its absence on an extract COA is a specific red flag — not a general concern, but a documented gap in the safety testing that extract products specifically require.

Jack Botanicals tests residual solvents as part of their 9-plus per batch protocol. Most extract vendors on the market do not.
